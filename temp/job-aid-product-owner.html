Business Observability — Product Owner Reference

Defines the business observability motivation chain and how to specify one for a capability you own.

What it is

Business observability connects what a capability's stakeholders expect to signals that show, in production, whether those expectations are being met.

A capability serves several stakeholders — customers, compliance, finance, operations — and each holds a different expectation. Business observability covers all of them, not only the customer-facing one.

The motivation chain

For each stakeholder, one chain:

who cares → what they expect → what we need to know → how we'd know → what's at risk

LinkQuestion it answers
StakeholderWho cares about this capability?
ExpectationWhat do they expect it to do?
NeedWhat do we need to know to confirm the expectation is met?
SignalHow would we know — what do we measure, and what is the target?
ImpactWhat is at risk if it stays unmet?

One expectation may decompose into more than one need (e.g. are payments completing? completing fast enough? absorbing volume spikes?). Each need gets its own signal.

Specifying it for your capability

For each stakeholder of your capability, walk the chain:

  1. Stakeholder — who cares, and how critical it is to them.
  2. Expectation — what they expect the capability to do.
  3. Need — what you need to know to confirm it. One expectation may yield more than one need.
  4. Signal — how you would know: the measure, its target, and the diagnostic signals to check when it fails.
  5. Impact — what is at risk, and in what unit, if it stays unmet.

Common error

Common error

Treating business observability as a single customer-facing outcome. A Tier 1 capability answers to several stakeholders — regulatory, financial, operational — whose expectations are not customer-facing at all. Walk the chain for each stakeholder, not just the customer. And state each need as a stakeholder expectation met, not as a system metric (uptime and latency are system health, not stakeholder outcomes).

Aggregation: a delivery-team PO holds the chains for one capability; a solution PO holds the set across capabilities; the product leader sees the portfolio.
