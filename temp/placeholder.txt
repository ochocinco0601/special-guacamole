Subject: Splunk Engineering Summit — Day 1 Recap (May 18, NYC)

Splunk's performance and stability issues have been impacting incident response across LOBs — slow searches, queuing, and outages that affect everyone on the MIM call at the same time. Critical fixes from Splunk slipped 4 months. abc escalated to Cisco leadership, and a 3-day engineering summit was convened in NYC this week to compress the timeline. PE and engineering representatives from across the LOBs are attending.

Search queuing: 12% today vs <1% target. 1.1 million searches run daily across all LOBs on a platform that can't keep up with the demand. When you run a search and it sits waiting, that's why.

The platform is being split by LOB. Today every LOB's workload runs on one shared cluster. A single hardware failure on May 5 degraded search for everyone for 90+ minutes. The analysis showed that different LOBs have very different usage patterns — CT is search-heavy at ~37% of search volume, DTI is data-heavy at ~90 TB/day. Separating them reduces the blast radius, improves performance for both, and brings each environment to a size Splunk supports well across other customers. The room agreed on the direction. CT's dedicated search environment is first, targeted for June 30.

Data replication — September target. Today, if one server has a problem, any data on that server becomes unsearchable. That's the root cause of the May 5 impact. The fix: every piece of data gets stored on two servers instead of one. Server failures stop cascading into platform-wide outages. This is also what makes four-nines availability possible — a requirement drove that reframed the engineering conversation for the rest of the day.

The September dates haven't moved yet. The fixes that triggered this escalation are still targeted for September 30. Days 2-3 will push on this.

No disaster recovery exists today. A backup environment is being built — partial capability by July, full DR by October. WF is building this ourselves because Splunk has no committed timeline for their native solution.

Platform visibility for LOB teams. I raised the need for a view that answers "is Splunk healthy right now?" from our perspective — not an infrastructure dashboard that requires understanding Splunk internals. Still being worked.

What's ahead: Whether September dates compress, the upgrade path, and reducing the impact of maintenance windows. Executive readout to leadership May 21.
